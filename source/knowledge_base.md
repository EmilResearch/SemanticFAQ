## SECTION 1: GLOBAL TRAVEL AND EXPENSE (GTE) POLICY

### 1.1 Air Travel Regulations
All corporate flights must be booked exclusively through the internal Globex Travel Portal (GTP). Bookings made via external consumer websites (e.g., Expedia, Booking.com) are strictly non-reimbursable unless the destination is not covered by the GTP database.
* **Advance Booking Window:** Flights must be booked at least 14 calendar days prior to the departure date. If a flight is booked less than 14 days in advance, an "Urgent Business Justification Form" (Form GTE-01) must be signed by the department Head.
* **Cabin Class:** Employees across all levels must book Economy Class for domestic flights and short-haul international flights. 
* **Business Class Exception:** Booking Business Class is permitted only if the continuous single-leg flight duration exceeds 8 hours **AND** the trip receives prior written approval from a Vice President (VP) or C-level executive.

### 1.2 Lodging and Accommodation Caps
Employees must select hotels classified as "Globex Preferred Partners" within the GTP. Hotel rates are subject to strict maximum nightly caps depending on the geopolitical tier of the destination city:
* **Tier 1 Cities** (New York, London, Tokyo, San Francisco, Zurich): Maximum $250.00 USD per night (excluding local taxes).
* **Tier 2 Cities** (Berlin, Sydney, Toronto, Rome, Singapore): Maximum $180.00 USD per night (excluding local taxes).
* **Standard Cities** (All other locations): Maximum $120.00 USD per night (excluding local taxes).
* **Over-Cap Procedure:** If no preferred hotel is available under the cap, the employee must obtain written clearance from the Finance Expense Team before confirming the reservation.

### 1.3 Meal Allowances and Per Diem Rates
Globex operates on a strict reimbursement-by-receipt model rather than a flat per-diem payout. The maximum daily allowance for meals is capped at $75.00 USD per day.
* **Individual Meal Limits:** Breakfast ($15.00), Lunch ($25.00), Dinner ($35.00). Unused balances from one meal cannot be rolled over to another.
* **Alcohol Policy:** Under no circumstances is alcohol reimbursable for standard corporate travel. If a dinner receipt contains alcohol, the employee must manually deduct the line item, adjust the tax percentage proportionally, and only claim the food items. 
* **Client Entertainment:** If an employee is hosting a client dinner, the limit is raised to $120.00 USD per person. Form GTE-09 (Client Entertainment Approval) must be attached, listing the names and company affiliations of all attendees.

### 1.4 Receipt Requirements and Lost Documentation
* **Threshold:** Itemized receipts are mandatory for any individual expense exceeding $25.00 USD. For expenses below $25.00 USD, a credit card statement line item is acceptable.
* **Itemization:** Standard credit card slips showing only the final total are not valid receipts. The full itemized bill showing exactly what was purchased is required.
* **Missing Receipts:** If a physical receipt is lost, the employee cannot use bank statements. They must fill out a "Missing Receipt Affidavit" (Form MRA-10), which requires a wet or digital signature from their direct line manager. No more than three MRA-10 forms may be submitted by a single employee within one calendar year.

### 1.5 Expense Submission Deadlines
All expense reports must be compiled and submitted through the Expensify portal within 30 calendar days following the final date of the business trip. 
* **Late Submissions:** Reports submitted between 31 and 60 days post-trip require a written warning review from HR. Reports submitted after 60 days are automatically locked out by the system, and the corporate credit card charges will be deducted directly from the employee's next payroll cycle.

## SECTION 2: IT SECURITY AND DATA PROTECTION POLICY

### 2.1 Password Management and Authentication
Security is paramount at Globex. All employees are issued a corporate Identity Account which governs access to internal networks, email, and cloud applications.
* **Password Complexity:** Passwords must be a minimum of 14 characters in length. They must contain at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, one numerical digit, and one special character (e.g., !, @, #, $).
* **Rotation Cycle:** Passwords automatically expire every 90 calendar days. System lockouts occur if the password is not updated prior to the expiration date. Reusing any of the previous 12 passwords is blocked by the system.
* **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** MFA via Okta Verify or a hardware YubiKey is mandatory for every login attempt originating from outside the corporate office network. SMS-based MFA is strictly prohibited due to SIM-swapping vulnerabilities.

### 2.2 Lost, Stolen, or Damaged Hardware
Corporate laptops, tablets, and mobile phones are the sole property of Globex Corporation.
* **Reporting Window:** If a corporate laptop or mobile device is lost or stolen, the employee must report the incident to the Security Operations Center (SOC) via phone (+1-555-SEC-HELP) or email (soc@globex.com) within 2 hours of discovering the loss. This triggers an immediate remote wipe command of the device.
* **Police Reports:** If the device was stolen in a public space, a police report must be filed within 24 hours, and the official case number must be forwarded to the IT Asset Management team.
* **Negligence Penalties:** If an investigation reveals the device was lost due to gross negligence (e.g., left in an unlocked vehicle overnight), the employee may be held financially liable for 50% of the hardware replacement cost.

### 2.3 Phishing and Suspicious Emails
Globex deploys automated email filtering, but sophisticated phishing attempts may still reach employee inboxes.
* **Reporting Protocol:** Employees must never reply to, click links within, or download attachments from suspicious emails. Instead, they must click the "Report Phishing" PhishMe button integrated into the Outlook toolbar.
* **Accidental Clicks:** If an employee accidentally clicks a link in a phishing email or enters their credentials, they must immediately disconnect their device from Wi-Fi and call the SOC emergency hotline within 15 minutes to isolate the machine from the corporate network.

### 2.4 Public Wi-Fi Restrictions
Working from cafes, airports, or hotels introduces severe sniffing and man-in-the-middle attack vectors.
* **VPN Mandate:** Employees are strictly forbidden from connecting to corporate applications over public, unsecured Wi-Fi networks unless the official Globex GlobalProtect VPN client is fully active and connected.
* **Tethering Alternative:** If the corporate VPN fails to connect, employees must use their corporate mobile phone as a secure cellular hotspot rather than remaining on unsecured public Wi-Fi.

## SECTION 3: REMOTE AND HYBRID WORK GUIDELINES

### 3.1 Core Collaboration Hours
Globex operates on a hybrid model allowing employees to balance remote work and in-office presence based on team agreements.
* **Core Hours:** Regardless of geographic time zones, all employees must be online, reachable, and available for synchronous meetings during the Core Collaboration Hours of 10:00 AM to 04:00 PM Eastern Standard Time (EST).
* **Status Updates:** Slack statuses must accurately reflect availability (e.g., "In a Meeting", "Lunch Break", "Deep Work"). Out-of-office (OOO) alerts must be set up in Outlook for any absence lasting longer than 4 consecutive hours during core periods.

### 3.2 Home Office Ergonomic Stipend
To ensure remote employees maintain a safe and productive working environment, Globex provides a one-time Home Office Equipment Stipend.
* **Stipend Amount:** A maximum of $500.00 USD is available to full-time employees who work remotely at least 3 days per week.
* **Eligible Items:** Ergonomic office chairs, standing desks, external monitors, keyboards, mice, and noise-canceling headsets.
* **Ineligible Items:** Decorative lighting, coffee machines, plants, home internet bills, or office stationery. Receipts must be submitted through Expensify using the category code "REMOTE-STIPEND-2026".

### 3.3 Internet Bandwidth Infrastructure Requirements
Employees working remotely are responsible for maintaining a high-quality residential internet connection capable of supporting video conferencing without degradation.
* **Minimum Speeds:** Remote workspaces must have a minimum dedicated internet connection speed of 50 Mbps download and 15 Mbps upload.
* **Testing:** IT Support may request a verified speed-test screenshot if an employee consistently suffers from connectivity drops or poor audio/video rendering during corporate calls.

### 3.4 Relocation and Cross-Border Remote Work
Remote work does not imply absolute geographic freedom due to complex state and international corporate tax compliance laws.
* **Notification Window:** Employees must notify HR and the Legal Tax Compliance Team in writing at least 30 days prior to relocating their permanent residential address.
* **Digital Nomad Limits:** Working from an international country outside of your primary employment contract jurisdiction is strictly limited to a maximum of 14 cumulative business days per calendar year. Exceeding this 14-day limit without a formal expat contract amendment is grounds for immediate termination.

## SECTION 4: PROCUREMENT AND EQUIPMENT PURCHASING

### 4.1 Software Licensing and Approvals
Employees are strictly prohibited from downloading, installing, or purchasing software tools for corporate use without official authorization.
* **Approval Pipeline:** Any new software, SaaS subscription, browser extension, or cloud tool must be vetted by the Architecture Review Board (ARB) and the Data Privacy Officer (DPO) prior to purchase.
* **Submission Portal:** Requests must be filed via Jira Service Management under the "Software Procurement" queue. The evaluation process takes up to 14 business days.
* **Unapproved Software:** Use of unauthorized software (Shadow IT) to store or process corporate data will result in immediate revocation of system access and disciplinary review.

### 4.2 Hardware Replacement Lifecycles
Globex ensures all personnel operate with modern, secure, and fully supported hardware assets.
* **Laptops:** Corporate laptops are placed on a mandatory 36-month replacement cycle. Upon reaching 3 years of service, the hardware asset system will automatically flag the device, and IT will ship a new pre-configured replacement.
* **Return of Old Hardware:** Employees must return their old laptop to the designated IT depot within 10 business days of receiving their new device. Failure to return old hardware will result in a suspension of the employee's single sign-on account.

### 4.3 Petty Cash and Micro-Purchases
For minor operational office supplies that cannot be processed via standard purchase orders, employees may utilize the local office petty cash fund.
* **Transaction Limit:** The maximum transaction amount for a petty cash reimbursement is $50.00 USD. Any purchase above this amount must go through a formal corporate credit card or a formal Purchase Order (PO).
* **Prohibited Petty Cash Items:** Software licenses, food/catering, client gifts, or recurring subscription payments.

## SECTION 5: CODE OF CONDUCT AND DISCIPLINARY PROCEEDINGS

### 5.1 Workplace Dress Code Standards
Globex maintains a professional environment tailored to the nature of an employee's day-to-day duties.
* **Standard Days:** The default dress code for all corporate offices is Smart Casual. This includes collared shirts, blouses, knitwear, chinos, and dark-wash denim without rips.
* **Client-Facing Events:** For client meetings, board presentations, and formal corporate events, the dress code elevates to Business Formal (suits, blazers, dress shirts, formal footwear).
* **Prohibited Attire:** Gym wear, sweatpants, beach sandals, flip-flops, and clothing containing offensive graphics or text are barred from the workplace at all times.

### 5.2 Social Media Policy and Public Disclosures
What employees post online can directly impact the market reputation and stock value of Globex Corporation.
* **Personal Accounts:** Employees must include a clear disclaimer in the bio or profile description of personal accounts (e.g., X, LinkedIn, Bluesky) if they publicly list Globex as their employer. The disclaimer must state: *"Views expressed are my own and do not represent my employer."*
* **Media Requests:** Employees are strictly forbidden from speaking to journalists, podcasters, or news outlets on behalf of Globex without explicit written sign-off from the VP of Corporate Communications. All incoming media inquiries must be forwarded immediately to press@globex.com.

### 5.3 Anti-Harassment and Reporting Hotlines
Globex is dedicated to providing a safe, inclusive, and respectful workplace free from harassment, bullying, and discrimination.
* **Reporting Channels:** Any employee who experiences or witnesses behavior violating this policy is urged to report it immediately. Reports can be made directly to their assigned HR Business Partner or anonymously via the Globex Ethics Integrity Hotline at +1-800-ETHICS-0.
* **Non-Retaliation:** Globex enforces a strict zero-tolerance policy for retaliation against any individual who reports misconduct in good faith. Investigation timelines are guaranteed to begin within 48 hours of report submission.
